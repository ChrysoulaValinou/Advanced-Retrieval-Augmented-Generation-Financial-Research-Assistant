## Executive Overview

TechVision Corp (TVC) delivered a record fiscal year 2024, driven by strong cloud infrastructure demand, expansion of its AI platform segment, and disciplined cost management. Total revenue grew **22.4% year-over-year** to **$4.87 billion**, while Non-GAAP operating income reached **$1.12 billion**, representing a margin of **23.0%**, up from 19.3% in FY2023.

The company ended the year with **$2.1 billion in cash and short-term investments**, no long-term debt, and a significantly strengthened free cash flow profile.

## Revenue by Segment

### 1. Cloud Infrastructure (47% of Revenue — $2.29B)

TechVision's cloud infrastructure segment remains the largest contributor to overall revenue. In FY2024, segment revenue increased **29.1%** year-over-year, benefiting from:

- A 35% increase in contracted cloud workloads from enterprise customers
- Geographic expansion into Southeast Asia, adding 11 new regional data center nodes
- Launch of **TVC HyperEdge 3.0**, which reduced latency by 40% and drove adoption among financial services clients

Key clients added during the year include four of the top ten global banks and two sovereign wealth funds.

### 2. AI & Analytics Platform (31% of Revenue — $1.51B)

The AI & Analytics Platform segment saw the highest growth in the portfolio, with revenue up **38.7%** year-over-year. This was driven by:

- Release of **TVC Nexus AI v4**, featuring multimodal reasoning and real-time data pipelines
- Expansion of the platform's enterprise API: 142,000 active developers as of December 2024 (up from 67,000 in December 2023)
- New strategic partnerships with Salesforce, SAP, and Oracle for embedded AI modules

Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) for the AI segment reached **$1.82B** at year-end, with a Net Revenue Retention rate of **128%**, indicating significant expansion within existing customers.

### 3. Software Licensing & Maintenance (14% of Revenue — $682M)

Legacy software licensing revenue declined **3.2%** as the company continues its deliberate migration strategy toward subscription models. Maintenance revenue held flat. The company expects this segment to be fully transitioned to SaaS by FY2026.

### 4. Professional Services (8% of Revenue — $390M)

Professional services revenue grew **11.8%**, supported by large cloud migration engagements and AI implementation projects. Backlog grew to **$510M** (up 28% YoY), providing strong visibility into FY2025.

## Q4 FY2024 Standalone Results

Q4 revenue reached **$1.31B**, growing **24.8% YoY**, the fourth consecutive quarter of accelerating growth. Non-GAAP operating income was **$312M** (margin: 23.8%).

Notable Q4 events:
- Signed a **$240M multi-year cloud deal** with a major European telecommunications company
- Completed the acquisition of **DataPulse Analytics** for $185M in cash, expanding TVC's real-time streaming analytics capabilities
- Repurchased **3.2 million shares** under the existing buyback authorization at an average price of $94.70

## Balance Sheet & Cash Flow

TechVision maintains a fortress balance sheet. As of December 31, 2024:

- **Cash, cash equivalents, and short-term investments:** $2.1B
- **Total debt:** $0 (fully repaid the $300M convertible note in March 2024)
- **Accounts receivable DSO:** 42 days (improved from 49 days in FY2023)
- **Capital expenditures:** $620M (primarily data center infrastructure and hardware)
- **Free Cash Flow:** $1.08B (FCF conversion: ~145% of GAAP net income)

The company authorized a new **$500M share repurchase program** in January 2025.

## Risk Factors

1. **Macroeconomic uncertainty**: A prolonged global slowdown could delay enterprise IT spending decisions
2. **Competitive pressure**: Hyperscalers (AWS, Azure, GCP) continue to expand into TVC's core markets
3. **Talent retention**: Competition for AI/ML engineers remains intense; compensation costs may rise
4. **Regulatory risk**: Proposed EU AI Act requirements could increase compliance costs in Europe
5. **Concentration risk**: Top 10 customers represent approximately 34% of total revenue
